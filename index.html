The Economic Impact of the South Korean Entertainment Industry

1. Introduction

The success of K-POP today is built on a systematic training process that produces high-caliber artists. In addition to holding regular auditions domestically, the company also organizes global competitions in countries to discover talented trainees—a strategy aimed at capturing international markets.

Moreover, K-POP leverages social media platforms like TikTok and Instagram Reels for rapid global exposure. Artists often initiate dance challenges to engage fans worldwide, further amplifying their reach.

2. The Rise and Global Influence of Korea Trend

K-pop: Hip hop and jazz influences, plus English lyrics, make K-pop more international.

Television Dramas: Korean dramas captivate audiences with engaging storylines and high-quality production.

Example: ROSÉ's debut solo album features full English songs. Born in New Zealand, raised in Australia, her background contributed to K-pop’s global expansion.

Films: Korean cinema is recognized for diverse themes and storytelling styles.

Cosmetics: Korean makeup trends, such as cushion foundations, thrive alongside entertainment culture.

Global Expansion: Collaborations with international artists and strong visuals expand Korean culture's influence.

3. The Economic Contributions of the Entertainment Industry

GDP Contribution:
2015: 6.4% of GDP.
2021: Output value of 39 trillion won (~NT$1.3 trillion), 6.57% of GDP.
2017-2021: Hallyu generated 37 trillion won in revenue.
Added value: 13.2 trillion won.

Export Growth:
Hallyu products grow 13.7% annually (2.5× South Korea’s average export rate).
2021 content exports: US$11.5 billion.

4. Government Policies and Support

K-POP is now the world's 6th largest music market. BTS's "Dynamite" brought an estimated 1.7 trillion won in economic benefits.

Strong CD and digital music market. Fans boost physical sales through fan culture. Idols also influence fashion and beauty markets.

Brand Influence: NewJeans and ROSÉ's endorsements boosted search trends for luxury brands across Asia. Example: Saint Laurent saw a 1,168% search increase in Indonesia post-promo.

Policy Support:
- Korean government funds festivals like KCON and MAMA.
- Encourages Netflix/YouTube collaborations.
- Supports Korean film and music globalization.
- Promotes UN collaborations for cultural influence.

5. Challenge and Conclusion

Mental Health: Intense training and schedules lead to burnout and anxiety. Better support is needed.

Market Centralization: Major agencies dominate, reducing indie opportunities and creativity.

Market Saturation: Fast content production leads to oversupply and viewer fatigue.
